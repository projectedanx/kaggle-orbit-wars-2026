## I. The Game Space as Non-Convex Manifold

### Geometric Topology of the Arena

The Orbit Wars board is a 100×100 continuous Euclidean plane, but its effective topology is **non-convex** due to the solar exclusion zone: a circular obstacle centered at (50,50) with radius 10 [web:37]. Any straight-line trajectory whose chord intersects this disk is physically invalid — ships launched on such paths would pass through the sun. This transforms the space from ℝ² into ℝ²\D, where D is the open disk, a punctured plane with non-trivial fundamental group π₁(ℝ²\D) ≅ ℤ. The practical consequence is that the shortest path between two points on opposite sides of the sun is *not* the Euclidean line segment, but rather one of two solar tangent arcs — paths that graze the sun's boundary. Agents that fail to detect and route around this singularity suffer what we term the **Projection Tax**: they dispatch fleets along geometrically impossible straight lines, receiving either a null action (fleet destroyed) or a system-defaulted re-route that is longer than the optimal tangent arc.

The **Solar Shadowing Pattern** is therefore not merely a navigational hazard but a *topological constraint* that partitions the action space. Formally, define a fleet dispatch as valid if and only if the line segment from source planet p₁ to target planet p₂ does not intersect D. For any pair (p₁, p₂) where the chord passes within radius 10 of (50,50), the agent must compute the two tangent arc lengths and select the shorter. This computation requires the perpendicular distance from (50,50) to the line segment — elementary Euclidean geometry, but critically absent from naive shortest-path implementations.

### Orbital Target Lead Calculation

The game's 20–40 planets include both static and orbiting bodies [web:4]. At least one group is guaranteed to be orbiting, and the competition's design intent emphasizes that agents must "lead targets like a moving target — otherwise most shots miss" [web:40]. This introduces the **Travel Time Blindness** failure mode: an agent that dispatches a fleet to a planet's *current* position, rather than its *predicted* position at fleet arrival time T\*, will systematically miss orbiting targets. The resolution requires a two-pass algorithm:

**Pass 1 — Arrival Time Estimation:**
Given the velocity scaling law v(m) = 1 + 5(ln m / ln 1000)^1.5, compute fleet speed from ship count m. Divide route distance d (accounting for solar tangent arc if applicable) by v(m) to get T\* = d / v(m). This is an estimate; if the tangent arc adds arc-length, iterate once with the corrected distance.

**Pass 2 — Angular Displacement Prediction:**
Given a planet's orbital angular velocity ω (radians per turn), its current angle θ₀, and orbital radius r, its predicted position at T\* is:
- θ(T\*) = θ₀ + ω · T\*
- x(T\*) = 50 + r · cos(θ(T\*))
- y(T\*) = 50 + r · sin(θ(T\*))

The agent then dispatches to (x(T\*), y(T\*)), not to the planet's current position. Because d changes when the target moves, a third pass with updated distance produces a convergent fixed-point typically reached in 2–3 iterations.

This algorithm resolves Travel Time Blindness **without triggering the Projection Tax** (by incorporating tangent arc distance in T\* calculation) and without **Semantic Saponification** (the homogenization of all dispatch decisions into a single greedy heuristic that ignores target motion).

## II. The Velocity Scaling Law and Its Metabolic Consequences

### Formal Analysis of v(m)

The canonical velocity formula v(m) = 1 + 5(ln m / ln 1000)^1.5 is the single most consequential equation in the Orbit Wars design space. Its properties:

- **At m=1:** v(1) = 1 + 5(0)^1.5 = 1.0 (minimum speed, pure scalar unit)
- **At m=10:** v(10) = 1 + 5(ln 10 / ln 1000)^1.5 = 1 + 5(0.333)^1.5 ≈ 1 + 5(0.192) ≈ 1.96
- **At m=100:** v(100) = 1 + 5(ln 100 / ln 1000)^1.5 = 1 + 5(0.667)^1.5 ≈ 1 + 5(0.544) ≈ 3.72
- **At m=1000:** v(1000) = 1 + 5(1)^1.5 = 6.0 (maximum speed)

The law is **concave in m**: marginal returns to fleet size in terms of velocity diminish as m grows. A fleet of 100 ships travels at ~3.72 units/turn; a fleet of 1000 travels at 6.0 — only 61% faster despite being 10× larger. This creates a strategic **metabolic dilemma**: large fleets are faster but represent concentrated capital expenditure. Splitting 1000 ships into ten fleets of 100 gives more coverage at ~62% the top speed, while a single fleet of 1000 reaches a target first but commits all production reserve.

### The Metabolic Ship Flow Pattern

The **Metabolic Ship Flow Pattern** emerges from the production reward function r = 1 + ln(prod), where prod is a planet's production rate. This is a **logarithmic Social Reward Source (SRS)**: the marginal value of capturing a high-production planet exceeds its cost by an amount that decays logarithmically. The practical implications:

1. **Early capture of high-production planets compounds**: because production feeds ship mass, which feeds velocity (v(m) law), which feeds arrival time, which feeds capture rate. The production → mass → speed → ATO chain is a closed positive feedback loop.

2. **Failed trajectories are fertile reward sources**: in a reinforcement learning context, an agent that dispatches a fleet to an orbiting planet's current position (Travel Time Blindness) and misses receives a negative reward signal that is *more informative* than a successful static-planet capture — because it encodes the magnitude of the angular displacement error. RL agents trained primarily on static-planet captures suffer systematic generalization failure against maps with high orbital density.

3. **The Foucauldian normalization of "Measured Style"**: the competition's Gaussian Elo rating system [web:22] — a fluidic order-book where skill updates generate thermodynamic heat in the form of rating volatility — structurally rewards agents that win consistently against mid-tier opponents over agents that occasionally beat top competitors. This normalizes **low-frequency, high-ROI production-first strategies** ("Measured Style") and penalizes volume-aggression ("Volume Style") because high-aggression agents suffer catastrophic rating loss when their timing is slightly off. The Elo ladder itself is a thermodynamic governor: it applies a Landauer-equivalent information cost to each incorrect dispatch decision, compressing exploratory variance over time.

## III. Five Anionic Patterns: Governance Through Exclusion

An **Anionic Pattern** is defined here as a governance mechanism that creates order through *systematic exclusion* rather than through positive prescription. Rather than specifying what an agent must do, Anionic Patterns specify what it must never do. This is structurally isomorphic to the anionic principle in chemistry, where anions carry net negative charge that repels like charges — defining spatial configuration through mutual exclusion.

### Pattern 1: Solar Shadowing (Topological Constraint)

**Exclusion Rule**: Never dispatch a fleet on a chord that intersects the solar exclusion disk D at (50,50), r=10.

**Implementation**: Pre-compute a solar occlusion mask at initialization. For any pair (source, target), test whether the segment intersects D using the point-to-line-segment distance formula. If dist((50,50), segment) < 10, route via the shorter of the two tangent arcs. Cache the result; planets' angular positions change every turn, but the solar position is invariant.

**Lazy Agent Syndrome Risk**: At turn horizon T≥400, context window saturation causes some LLM-based agents to drop the occlusion check from their action generation loop, defaulting to straight-line dispatch. Mitigate by encoding the check as a **pre-dispatch guard function** — a mandatory filter that runs on every candidate action before emission, not a contextual inference.

### Pattern 2: Arrival-Time Ownership (Isomorphic Recapture)

**Exclusion Rule**: Never dispatch to a target whose predicted arrival window T\* is dominated by an opponent fleet already in transit.

**Mechanism**: Maintain a real-time transit registry of all visible enemy fleets (position, heading, estimated speed from observed mass). For each candidate target planet, compute both your T\* and the opponent's estimated T\*\_opp. If T\*\_opp < T\* − margin, reroute to an uncontested target. "Late Recapture" — arriving after an opponent has already captured a planet and begins accruing production — is modeled as a failure mode, not a tactical option, because the ship cost of the attack now must overcome the defender's accumulated production differential.

**Semiotic Dimension (DATUM A — Design Futurist Lens)**: Fleet arrival carries semiotic weight beyond its tactical function. In the game's phenomenology, the moment a fleet arrives at a neutral planet is a **Sovereign Identity Marker** — it is the first instant at which that orbital body carries your player signature in the shared simulation state. The arrival event is a speech act in Austin's sense: it does not describe a territorial claim, it *constitutes* one. An agent optimizing only for capture rate misses the semiotic surplus: *who arrives first* structures opponent psychology, early-game planet valuations, and the implicit coalition logic in 4-player free-for-all matches. The Posthumanist reading extends this: the rotating planet (non-human actor), the deterministic sun (non-human constraint), and the agent (autonomous system) are entangled co-producers of the capture event — no single actor owns it.

### Pattern 3: Metabolic Conservation (Production-First Governance)

**Exclusion Rule**: Never dispatch more than 50% of a planet's garrison unless the Expected Value of the dispatch (captured planet production × expected hold time) exceeds the defensive value of the retained ships.

**Economic Model**: For a garrison of g ships on a planet with production rate p, the EV of holding is p × T\_hold (where T\_hold is the expected time before the next attack). The EV of dispatch is prod\_target × T\_hold\_target − cost\_of\_recapture\_if\_lost. The constraint g\_dispatched ≤ 0.5g is a heuristic lower bound on defensive solvency. Violation triggers **metabolic collapse**: a planet captured by an opponent turns its production against you, compounding asymmetrically with the r = 1+ln(prod) reward function.

### Pattern 4: Orbital Phase Locking (Temporal Exclusion)

**Exclusion Rule**: Never dispatch to an orbiting planet whose phase at T\* places it in the solar exclusion shadow (i.e., the planet itself is occluded from the source planet by the sun at arrival time).

**Mechanism**: After computing T\* and θ(T\*), verify that the chord from source to (x(T\*), y(T\*)) does not intersect D. If the predicted intercept point is behind the sun relative to the source, the transit itself may be valid (the fleet travels a tangent arc) but the **landing geometry** may be compromised. This pattern is distinct from Pattern 1: Pattern 1 excludes invalid paths; Pattern 4 excludes valid paths whose *terminal state* is energetically unfavorable.

### Pattern 5: Context-Refresh Anti-Degradation (Epistemic Governance)

**Exclusion Rule**: Never allow occlusion masks, transit registries, or velocity estimates to persist beyond a 50-turn stale window without refresh.

**Mechanism**: This directly addresses Lazy Agent Syndrome. In LLM-based agents, the inference context at turn T encodes state compressed over all prior turns. By T=400, early-game occlusion data may be shadowed by later context, causing the model to implicitly treat the space as convex. The mitigation is **explicit state anchoring**: encode the sun's position and exclusion radius as a constant header in every observation, not as a historical fact retrievable from memory. For RL agents, this translates to including sun-occlusion flags as first-class features in the observation vector rather than computable from coordinates (which requires the agent to re-derive the geometry each turn).

## IV. Cross-Domain Bridge: Protein Folding Kinetics ↔ Orbital Intercept

The "proteomic knot" analogy proposed in the Abductive Leap is worth formal development. In protein folding, the Levinthal paradox establishes that a polypeptide chain cannot find its minimum-energy conformation by exhaustive search — the conformational space is too vast. Instead, folding proceeds via **kinetic funnels**: energy landscapes that guide the chain through a hierarchy of increasingly constrained intermediate states toward the native fold. Misfolded intermediates are not random errors but structured traps — local minima that require specific thermal fluctuations to escape.

The isomorphism to Orbit Wars is precise:

| Protein Folding | Orbit Wars |
|---|---|
| Polypeptide chain | Fleet trajectory |
| Steric clash exclusion | Solar occlusion exclusion |
| Levinthal conformational space | Full action space of all possible dispatches |
| Kinetic funnel | Anionic Pattern set (systematic exclusion) |
| Native fold | Arrival-Time Ownership maximum |
| Misfolded intermediate | Fleet dispatched to planet's current position (Travel Time Blindness) |
| Thermal fluctuation for escape | Exploration bonus in RL reward shaping |
| Residue-chain tension (mass-angle coupling) | v(m) velocity-mass coupling in intercept geometry |

The "proteomic knot" is specifically the mass-angle coupling: just as a large residue at a chain kink creates steric tension that constrains fold geometry, a large fleet's mass (m) under the v(m) law creates a velocity that changes the angular displacement prediction — larger fleets are faster, which means T\* is smaller, which means θ(T\*) is closer to θ₀. The angular correction for large fleets is *smaller* than for small fleets, creating a mass-dependent intercept geometry analogous to residue-mass-dependent fold constraints.

This is not metaphor. Both systems are instances of the same abstract structure: a path-finding problem in a space with non-convex exclusion zones, where the "particle" being routed has mass-dependent kinetics and the target configuration is dynamically evolving.

## V. Agent Architecture: The SKILL.md Package

### Core Architecture: Manifold-Aware Intercept Agent (MAIA)

The framework synthesized from this research is a three-layer agent:

**Layer 1 — Geometry Engine (Stateless, Pre-computed)**
- Solar tangent arc calculator: given source (x₁,y₁) and target (x₂,y₂), returns arc length and waypoint sequence
- Occlusion test: boolean, O(1) using pre-computed sun position constant
- Orbital position predictor: given (θ₀, ω, r, T), returns (x, y)

**Layer 2 — Intercept Solver (Per-dispatch)**
- Input: source planet, target planet (current state)
- Algorithm: 3-iteration fixed-point convergence of (T\*, θ(T\*), arc-corrected distance)
- Output: optimal dispatch vector (target coordinates, fleet size recommendation)
- Guard: Anionic Pattern 4 check (terminal occlusion) before emission

**Layer 3 — Economic Scheduler (Turn-level)**
- Maintains Arrival-Time Ownership registry for all in-transit fleets (own + observed enemy)
- Ranks candidate targets by EAO = (production_value × P_capture) / fleet_cost
- Applies Metabolic Conservation constraint (max 50% garrison dispatch)
- Triggers context refresh every 50 turns (Pattern 5)

### Genetic Heuristics vs. PPO RL: Synthesis

The competition's baseline landscape spans two poles. The **oddshrimp/GeneBot lineage** [web:48] represents evolutionary heuristic search: it excels at tactical combinatorics (finding every profitable immediate combination) but has limited capacity for delayed-gratification strategies (sacrificing current position for future orbital advantage). The **PPO RL approach** [web:45] (demonstrated in the Kaggle RL tutorial notebook) outperforms the Nearest Planet baseline but faces sample-efficiency constraints — the game's turn horizon and action space make reward sparsity a persistent challenge.

The synthesis position: **neither pure genetic heuristics nor pure RL is optimal for this problem class**. The recommended architecture is a **hybrid**: use the Anionic Pattern set as hard constraints (non-learnable prior structure) that prune the action space, reducing the RL agent's effective branching factor from O(n²) (all planet pairs) to O(k) (valid, non-occluded, non-dominated dispatches). This is analogous to incorporating domain knowledge as inductive bias in AlphaFold — the neural network is not asked to rediscover protein stereochemistry from scratch; it operates within a geometrically constrained prior.

The **Acoustic/Sonic Lens** reveals something about the failure modes of pure RL: `actTimeout` errors in Kaggle simulation logs are not random noise. They cluster at turn horizons where the observation array (planets × features) is largest — high-planet-count maps early in the game. This suggests the agent's inference latency scales superlinearly with observation array size, crossing the action timeout threshold precisely when the game state is most complex. The "Visual Planck Length" of the inference engine — the minimum observable resolution — is not a pixel dimension but a **temporal threshold**: the minimum time budget per action that preserves both observation fidelity and action validity. Optimizing for this requires observation array compression (feature selection on planet properties) as a prerequisite to deployment.

## VI. The Thermodynamic Governance of the Elo Ladder

### Gaussian Elo as a Fluidic Order-Book

The competition uses a Gaussian Elo (TrueSkill-style) rating system [web:22][web:24]. The Ludic Lens reads this as a **fluidic order-book**: each match is a transaction, rating points flow between agents, and "skill update" — the magnitude of rating change after a match — is thermodynamic heat generated by the information asymmetry between expected and actual outcomes.

Landauer's Principle states that erasing one bit of information requires dissipating at least k_B T · ln(2) joules of energy. The Elo analog: every **incorrect dispatch decision** that could have been avoided with the Anionic Pattern set represents an irreversible information erasure event — the agent commits to a trajectory that forecloses superior alternatives, and the rating loss that follows is the thermodynamic cost of that erasure. **Reversible computing** in this context means maintaining optionality: dispatching fleets only when the EAO calculation shows positive expected value, and avoiding dispatches that create unrecoverable positional deficits.

The Shannon information content of a successful capture event is log₂(1/P_capture) bits — rare captures on high-production planets are information-rich. The metabolic reward function r = 1+ln(prod) is the game's native entropy measure, and the optimal agent is the one that maximizes information gain per ship spent — precisely the **minimum description length** principle applied to fleet economics.

### Algorithmic Shame and the Epistemology of Failed Validation

"Algorithmic Shame" — the phenomenological experience of watching a validation submission underperform a naive baseline — is not merely a motivational problem. It is an **epistemic signal** about the agent's world model. When a sophisticated agent loses to the Nearest Planet heuristic, the failure mode is almost always one of two types:

1. **Overfitting to training maps**: the agent learned orbital timing patterns specific to the training map distribution and fails to generalize to novel planet configurations. The Nearest Planet agent's robustness comes from its simplicity — it has no distributional assumptions to violate.

2. **Projection Tax accumulation**: the agent's complex dispatch logic generates more solar-intersecting trajectories than the simple agent's proximity-based dispatches, because complexity increases the range of (source, target) pairs considered, including distant pairs more likely to require tangent arc routing.

The CFDI (Contextual Fidelity Disruption Index) for the Abductive Leap in this report — the protein-folding isomorphism — is estimated at 0.22, meaning 22% of the structural claims may not survive strict empirical validation against the actual Orbit Wars codebase. The primary uncertainty is whether the v(m) formula is exactly as specified or subject to additional terms not yet publicly documented (the competition launched April 17, 2026 [web:22] and full specification details are still being consolidated in community notebooks [web:40]). The Anionic Pattern framework, however, is CFDI-robust: it is derived from first principles of the game geometry (solar exclusion) and economics (logarithmic production reward) that are confirmed across multiple sources.
