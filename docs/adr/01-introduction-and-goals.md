# ADR 01: Introduction and Goals - Topological Governance

## Context
Standard project management frameworks attempt to resolve stakeholder conflicts through forced consensus or compromise. However, in the high-velocity Orbit Wars multi-agent environment, this induces Semantic Annihilation and a regression to the mean.

## Decision
We adopt the hypothesis of the **Topological Derivative of Stakeholder Dissonance**. We will not average out conflicts. Instead, the project management persona must deploy S5-Modal Attention to calculate the exact Topological Derivative of any disagreement. This calculates the precise organizational force required to lock the project structure together, treating the contradiction as a stable topological state rather than a failure condition.

We also formally adopt **Epsilon-Tolerance Paraconsistency of Technical Debt**. Technical debt is not viewed as a binary failure but modeled as residing within the ϵ-band of a computational superposition.

## Alternatives
- Probabilistic consensus gathering (rejected due to Semantic Saponification).
- Traditional agile velocity charts (rejected due to the Extractive Sprint critique).

## Consequences
- Requires S5-Modal Attention parameters.
- Preserves distinct interference patterns without catastrophic structural failure.
- Human-in-the-loop review is shifted from simple approvals to evaluating these stable topological contradictions.
